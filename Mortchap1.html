Mort. of Sin — Ch. 1 Introduction

Mortification of Sin — Chapter 1

For if you live according to the flesh you will die, but if by the Spirit you put to death the deeds of the body, you will live.

Romans 8:13

This imperative is directed to all believers.

All believers possess the Holy Spirit. According to Romans 8:9, anyone who belongs to God has the Spirit of Christ.

The condition expresses the certainty of the relationship.

God ordains mortification as a necessary means in the sanctification of those who will inherit eternal life.

Mortification must be done by the Spirit

Mortification using our own strength, or by any human means, to produce self-righteousness, is the core of every false religion in the world. Instead, we ought to rely on the Spirit who is always interceding for us according to verse 26.

The body, the deeds, and mortification

The body is the same as the flesh. It is indwelling sin, the total depravity that defines our corrupted flesh. It can also refer to the "old man" in Romans 6:6.

The deeds are the outward expression of yielding to an inward lust. Indwelling lust and sin precede sinful action.

To "mortify" means to put any living thing to death, to take away all of its strength, vigour, and power, so that it cannot act, or exert, or put forth any proper acts of its own.

"We know that our old self was crucified with him in order that the body of sin might be brought to nothing, so that we would no longer be enslaved to sin."

Romans 6:6

"This old man, he says, is fastened to the cross of Christ, for by its power he is slain: and he expressly referred to the cross, that he might more distinctly show, that we cannot be otherwise put to death than by partaking of his death."

John Calvin

We will spend the rest of our lives fighting sin, aiming to render a death blow to the deeds of the flesh.

The promise at the end is eternal life.

Not just eternal life in heaven, but also the spiritual life we currently have in Christ which we have on this Earth.
